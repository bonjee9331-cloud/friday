# Brand viability verdict on five candidate names

**None of the five primary candidates is a clean winner.** HOW2SELL and HOW TO SELL are eliminated: the .com of each is held by an active direct competitor in sales training, and the phrases are effectively unregistrable as word marks under USPTO, UKIPO, and EUIPO descriptiveness doctrine. GENERATE BUSINESS faces absolute-grounds refusal risk in Class 35 plus opposition exposure from senior UK marketing brands. GENBIZ collides with an active decade-old US nonprofit at genbiz.com and at least four other SMB operators. THE BUSINESS FORGE is the only candidate with a defensible trademark path, but it sits in a saturated "-forge" space with an already-active UK site at businessforge.co.uk, a US podcast at thebusinessforge.us, and a CSU Monterey Bay incubator using the identical mark. **The recommendation is to return to the prior shortlist (Clientwright, Clientry, Clientbound) and consider "How2Sell Academy" as a product-tier label underneath it.**

Below is the full deep dive per name. Research was conducted by specialist subagents on 19 April 2026. Live registry portals (WHOIS, USPTO TSDR, UKIPO, EUIPO, CIPO, IP Australia, Thailand DIP) and social profile pages are JavaScript-gated or bot-blocked, so items that could not be verified directly are flagged "UNABLE TO VERIFY" and require a clearance attorney to confirm before any filing decision. All factual claims are cited inline.

### G) Descriptive-refusal risk

Applied to HOW2SELL in Class 41 sales training, the phonetic equivalent is "how to sell," which directly describes the subject matter. Expected outcomes: **§2(e)(1) merely descriptive refusal** at USPTO, **s.3(1)(c) refusal** at UKIPO, **Art 7(1)(c) refusal** at EUIPO. Principal Register path would require §2(f) acquired distinctiveness (typically five years of substantially exclusive use) which is closed because how2sell.com and other operators already use the phrase commercially. Supplemental Register or logo-with-disclaimer is the best realistic outcome; neither gives exclusive rights in the wording.

### G) Descriptive-refusal and confusion risk
**Distinctiveness**: "Forge" used figuratively for "to shape/create" is metaphorical and classically suggestive. Under **TMEP §1209.01(a)**, suggestive marks are inherently distinctive and registrable without acquired distinctiveness. Composite "THE BUSINESS FORGE" reads as a unitary suggestive phrase; USPTO should accept it with a likely disclaimer of "BUSINESS." UKIPO s.3(1) and EUIPO Art 7(1)(b)/(c) should pass on absolute grounds with similar disclaimer practice. **Verdict on absolute grounds: registrable as a word mark.**

**Relative grounds (likelihood of confusion)** is the real risk. Under **TMEP §1207.01** du Pont factors applied to the Salesforge family:
- **Similarity of marks**: The dominant "-FORGE" suffix triggers family-of-marks analysis, but the leading noun (BUSINESS vs SALES) differs meaningfully, and the "The ___ Forge" syntax differs from SalesForge's closed-compound pattern. Moderately favourable for coexistence.
- **Similarity of goods/services**: HIGHEST-RISK FACTOR. SalesForge occupies Class 9/42 SaaS plus Class 35 sales-outreach services. A UK premium education brand naturally files in **Class 41 (education/training)** and Class 35 (business consulting). If the applicant avoids claiming Class 9 downloadable software or Class 42 SaaS for the "Bonnie" product under the "Business Forge" banner, services are materially different. If Bonnie is marketed under the same umbrella in Class 9/42, it collides.
- **Sophistication of buyers**: Premium B2B training buyers will not confuse a training academy with a cold-email SaaS. Favourable.
- **Crowded field**: "-FORGE" is demonstrably crowded (see *Juice Generation, Inc. v. GS Enters. LLC*, 794 F.3d 1334 (Fed. Cir. 2015)), which weakens every individual "-forge" mark's protective scope. Cuts both ways.

**Conclusion**: **Registrable narrowly in Class 41 and Class 35**, provided the AI software product Bonnie is filed separately as its own distinctive mark in Class 9/42 rather than under "Business Forge." The crowded "-forge" landscape is a **branding risk** (dilution, SEO confusion) even when the legal TM risk is manageable. The US senior users of the identical mark (Division 42 podcast, CSU incubator, Small Business Forge) create separate §2(d) exposure specific to "THE BUSINESS FORGE" that does not apply to more differentiated "Business Forge" variants.
